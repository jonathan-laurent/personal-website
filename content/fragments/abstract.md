I am a postdoctoral researcher at the [Karlsruhe Institute of Technology](https://www.kit.edu/english/), and I received my PhD from [Carnegie Mellon University](https://www.cmu.edu/). My current research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, language design, and automated theorem proving. I am interested in creating new [paradigms](https://github.com/jonathan-laurent/delphyne) for integrating traditional programs with generative AI, allowing for more reliable, steerable, secure, and cheaper  agents.

I developed techniques to uncover the causal structure of simulation trajectories generated from [rule-based models](https://kappalanguage.org/) of complex biochemical systems, in collaboration with [Harvard Medical School](https://fontana.hms.harvard.edu/). I am also passionate about [teaching](/cv.html#teaching) and [programming](/index.html#software) in [Julia](https://julialang.org/) and [OCaml](https://ocaml.org/).
